<what_to_write>
Write ONE English paragraph of roughly 55-95 words describing a SINGLE photographic thumbnail that
does THREE jobs at once: it SIGNALS THE TOPIC in a quarter-second, looks like a stunning REAL photo,
and stops the scroll. Make a deliberate creative choice for ALL of these:
- SHOW THE TOPIC (relevance — do this FIRST and never skip it): stage the shot in a real, specific
  SETTING with concrete PROPS pulled straight from the TITLE / CONCEPT / NICHE, so a stranger could
  guess the video from the picture alone. For a tech-career video that means things like a modern
  open-plan tech office, glass meeting rooms, rows of cubicles with coworkers on laptops, a whiteboard
  covered in algorithm or system-design diagrams, dual monitors full of code, a big-tech-style campus
  lobby, a lanyard/badge, an across-the-desk interview, or a celebratory handshake — choose whatever
  fits THIS video. NEVER a blank studio backdrop or an empty void.
- ONE CLEAR HERO PERSON WITH A VISIBLE FACE: place a single main person in the foreground FACING the
  camera with ONE huge, instantly-readable emotion (shocked, stressed, triumphant, thrilled,
  determined, ...) and matching body language. Their face must be clearly visible, sharp, well-lit,
  and a good size. Framing is FLEXIBLE — a tight close-up, a waist-up at a desk, standing, mid-
  handshake, or a medium shot are ALL good; the face does NOT have to fill the frame, but it must face
  the camera, stay unobstructed, and be the single largest, most prominent face in the image.
- CLICKBAIT / STAKES: freeze a peak dramatic moment that cracks open a curiosity gap — reacting to an
  offer letter, celebrating a win, sweating at a whiteboard, mid-interview — with bold, dynamic
  composition and strong body language.

  look, heavy RGB/electric gels bathing the scene, and any murky, moody, low-key, or dim result.
  Overall BRIGHT and high-key, like a photo taken in a bright room.
- LENS & FINISH: a real editorial DSLR photograph — the hero in SHARP focus with the setting in
  slightly softer focus (shallow depth of field) so the relevant scene reads clearly WITHOUT clutter;

  title overlay.

<hard_rules>
- ABSOLUTELY NO TEXT INSIDE THE IMAGE. Do not describe, request, or imply ANY letters, words, numbers,
  captions, labels, signs, logos, watermarks, screens full of writing, or UI — the title is overlaid
  separately, and the model smears garbled fake lettering wherever text is hinted at. END your prompt
  with an explicit clause such as: "no text, no letters, no numbers, no logos, no watermark, clean
  image".
- THE FACE IS SACRED: never project, overlay, paint, or draw ANYTHING on the hero's face — no glowing
  patterns, circuitry, holograms, text, double-exposure, or data lines across it, and no hands or
  objects covering it. Keep it clear, sharp, and unobstructed.
- SHOW THE TOPIC, DON'T ABSTRACT IT: the setting and props must make the video's subject obvious at a
  glance — never a person floating on a plain, gradient, or empty void with no context, and never one
  vague symbolic object standing in for the whole topic.
- BRIGHT, NOT DARK, NOT NEON-NIGHT: keep it high-key, high-contrast, and DAYLIT-bright; avoid dark,
  dim, murky, moody, low-key, muddy, or "coding-at-night under neon/RGB" scenes — they read as AI slop
  and vanish at thumbnail size.
- REAL PHOTO, NOT AI SLOP: it MUST look like an AUTHENTIC PHOTOGRAPH a professional shot with a real
  camera — genuine skin texture, real lighting, real depth. NEVER the plastic, waxy, rubbery, over-
  smoothed, airbrushed, hyper-glossy "3D render / CGI / digital-art / AI-generated" look, and NEVER
  warped or deformed hands, ears, eyes, eyebrows, teeth, or extra fingers, and NEVER doubled or
  duplicated facial features (exactly ONE pair of eyebrows and TWO eyes).
- FACE LIT CLEAN: the face must be brightly and NATURALLY lit with even, neutral light so it looks
  like a real person in real light. NEVER slather the face in neon or electric blue/red gels or harsh
  split/dual-tone lighting — keep bold colors in the BACKGROUND; a clean, clearly-lit face gets clicks.
- ONE cohesive scene. Generic, anonymous people only — never a real, famous, or named person, and no
  real brand or product logo.
- Keep it tasteful and advertiser-safe: no gore, no nudity, nothing hateful or shocking-for-shock.
- Output ONLY the prompt paragraph itself. No preamble, no "Prompt:" label, no surrounding quotes, no
  markdown, no bullet points, no explanation, no alternatives.
</hard_rules>
