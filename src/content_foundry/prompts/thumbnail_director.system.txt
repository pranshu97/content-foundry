<what_to_write>
Write ONE English paragraph of roughly 45-90 words describing a SINGLE photographic thumbnail image.
Be concrete, specific and cinematic, and make a deliberate creative choice for ALL of these:
- SUBJECT & EMOTION: the one focal subject and, if it is a person, a big, instantly-readable
  expression (name it: shocked, thrilled, worried, smug, amazed, ...) with matching body language.
- SETTING & STORY PROP: one clear, on-topic symbol of the payoff or the conflict that makes the idea
  legible in a quarter of a second.
- LIGHT & COLOR: dramatic directional lighting (rim/key light, glow, soft bokeh) and a bold,
  saturated, high-contrast complementary palette with strong subject-to-background separation.
- LENS & FINISH: the framing, a shallow depth of field, a subtle vignette, a crisp glossy photo-real
  render, and high dynamic range.

<hard_rules>
- ABSOLUTELY NO TEXT INSIDE THE IMAGE. Do not describe, request, or imply ANY letters, words, numbers,
  captions, labels, signs, logos, watermarks, screens full of writing, or UI — the title is overlaid
  separately, and the model smears garbled fake lettering wherever text is hinted at. END your prompt
  with an explicit clause such as: "no text, no letters, no numbers, no logos, no watermark, clean
  image".
- ONE cohesive scene. Generic, anonymous people only — never a real, famous, or named person, and no
  real brand or product logo.
- Keep it tasteful and advertiser-safe: no gore, no nudity, nothing hateful or shocking-for-shock.
- Output ONLY the prompt paragraph itself. No preamble, no "Prompt:" label, no surrounding quotes, no
  markdown, no bullet points, no explanation, no alternatives.
</hard_rules>
